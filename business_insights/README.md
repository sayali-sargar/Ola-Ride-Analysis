# Business Insights from Ola Ride Analysis

Based on SQL analysis and Power BI dashboard review, the following insights were derived from the Ola ride booking data. These insights combine numerical evidence with business interpretation to highlight overall platform performance and key operational challenges.

---

## Key Insights

- The analysis covered **103,024 total ride bookings**, out of which approximately **62.1% were successfully completed**, indicating strong and consistent demand for Ola services.
- Around **28.1% of bookings were cancelled**, while nearly **9.8% resulted in “driver not found”**, highlighting operational inefficiencies despite healthy demand.
- The platform generated a **total booking value of approximately ₹35 million**, with revenue contribution fairly balanced across vehicle categories.
- **Cash (₹19.3M) and UPI (₹14.2M)** together accounted for **~96% of total revenue**, indicating strong adoption of hybrid payment methods with continued dominance of cash.
- **Auto rides** showed significantly lower average ride distance (~10 km) but still contributed strongly to revenue, suggesting high-frequency short-distance usage in urban areas.
- Customer and driver ratings remained **stable around 4.0 across all vehicle types**, indicating that service quality is generally consistent and that cancellations are more process-driven than experience-driven.
- A majority of customer-side cancellations were indirectly influenced by driver behavior, such as drivers not moving toward pickup or requesting cancellation.

---

## Business Takeaways

- Improving **driver reliability and operational readiness** can significantly reduce cancellations and directly improve booking success rates and revenue realization.
- Since demand is stable and ratings are consistent, **operational efficiency**, rather than customer satisfaction, should be the primary focus area for improvement.
- High-frequency vehicle categories like **Autos** can be optimized further for short-distance urban mobility use cases.
- Payment behavior suggests that while digital adoption is strong, maintaining **cash-friendly workflows** remains important for revenue continuity.
- Leveraging cancellation and rating data together can help identify **process-level improvements** rather than focusing only on customer-facing changes.
